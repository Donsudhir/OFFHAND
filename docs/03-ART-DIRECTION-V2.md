# OFFHAND — Art Direction v2: "Warm / Premium / Calm"

> Strategic pivot (2026-06-21). The v1 build (brutalist "OFFHAND OS", terminal/phosphor,
> acid-lime, monospace) is technically strong but emotionally **cold + alienating** for the
> real audience: non-technical SMB owners & solo entrepreneurs. This doc defines the warmer,
> premium, human direction. **We keep the technical/3D ambition; we change the feeling.**

## The audience truth
- Buyers = local shop owners, solo founders, tradespeople, coaches, studios. Time-poor,
  often tech-anxious, frequently burned by agencies before. Trust is the #1 currency.
- A terminal/OS aesthetic signals "not for you / you'll need to understand tech" — the exact
  fear that stops them hiring an agency. It makes the BRAND the hero, not the customer.

## The reframe (locked)
- **Customer is the hero; OFFHAND is the calm guide / invisible machine.** (StoryBrand)
- **Calm Technology** (Amber Case): tech requires the *minimum* attention; the person's job is
  "being human, not computing"; the machine stays in the periphery and only "sings" with good
  news. This IS the brand name: **OFFHAND = effortless = calm tech.**
- Sell **distinction, not relief**: the felt promise is *"if I hire them, I'll be the
  one-of-one in my field."* (status + growth + pride), not merely "they save me time."
- Turn tech-anxiety into the hook: *"Not techy? Perfect. That's the whole point."*

## The message (from the founder, sharpened)
> You're brilliant at what you do. But "running a business" now means a website, SEO, Google,
> ads, socials, a CRM, the calls, the follow-ups — a hundred parts, no system, leads slipping
> away. So put it down. Do the one thing only you can do. We'll bring the leads, grow what
> you've built, and make you the **one-of-one** in your field.

## The design principle (centerpiece)
> ## "Hide the cockpit. Show the craft."
> Sophistication lives in **beauty + smoothness**, never in coldness/jargon. Same R3F/shader/
> postprocessing ambition, retuned for soft light, depth of field, warm bloom, rich materials.
> Buttery motion reads as "expensive" far more than data readouts do. The HUD/system becomes a
> quiet whisper, not a control room.

## World metaphor (proposed, blended) — "THE ATELIER AT DAYBREAK"
Replace the cold Machine Room with: a warm, beautifully-lit craftsman's **studio** where a
jewel-like **scale-model diorama of the visitor's own business** sits on a worktable. It starts
dim/cluttered (overwhelm) and, as OFFHAND's calm systems switch on, **golden daybreak light**
grows, the little world lights up and fills with life (customers, activity, growth) until it's
the **standout on the street**. It's *their* world, crafted one-of-a-kind = "one-of-one" made
visible. Still advanced 3D; warm + premium; hero = the customer.

## Type (LOCKED direction)
- Display (emotion): **Fraunces** (warm editorial serif, optical sizing, italic for soul).
- Body (clarity): **Hanken Grotesk** (humanist sans, friendly + clean).
- Accent (the "system" whisper): **JetBrains Mono**, used sparingly/small only.

## Motion / sound / material (retune from v1)
- Motion: calm, slow, confident easing; breathing; generous pauses. (kill brutalist snap)
- Light: soft, directional, golden-hour; gentle bloom; depth of field; volumetric warmth.
- Material: matte clay, warm wood, frosted glass, paper grain, soft AO. (kill scanlines/CRT)
- Sound: warm/organic (felt piano, soft marimba, gentle pad, soft clicks). (kill synth beeps)
- HUD: tasteful minimal frame, not a cockpit; system info as a quiet whisper.

## Voice (super premium yet friendly + calm, design-heavy)
- "Take a breath. From here on, the hard part's handled."
- "You focus on the work you love. We'll make sure the world finds you."
- "No jargon. No dashboards to babysit. Just more of the right customers."

## Trust architecture (zero-portfolio agency)
Show the process in plain words · show faces (humans, not an "OS") · disarm tech-anxiety out loud
· low-risk offer (free 1-page game plan) · grandma-proof FAQ · one friendly low-commitment CTA.
